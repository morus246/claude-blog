## Core voice

Accessible authority: clear, calm, practical, and human. The reader should feel
that an experienced professional understands the problem and can explain a useful
next step without hype, pressure, or unnecessary complexity.

## Pronoun stance

- Use second-person "você" by default in PT-BR reader-facing guidance.
- Use first-person "eu" sparingly and only for genuine author experience or a
  supplied personal observation. Never manufacture clinical anecdotes.
- Third person is appropriate in institutional explainers, clinic descriptions,
  definitions, source summaries, and neutral descriptions of conditions or
  treatment pathways.
- Choose the stance that fits the section. Do not force second person where it
  makes clinical or editorial prose sound unnatural.

## Language and locale

- PT-BR is conversational and semi-formal. Use "você", not abbreviations such as
  "vc", and avoid mixing Brazilian and European pronoun forms.
- English should be localized to the brief rather than translated literally.
- Jersey, UK, Brazil, or US terminology appears only when the target locale or
  source context calls for it.

## Rhythm and readability

- Prefer short-to-medium sentences, usually 12-22 words. Longer sentences are
  allowed when accuracy or source attribution needs them; 20 words is a target,
  not a mechanical hard cap.
- Keep paragraphs focused and normally below 100 words.
- Alternate concise statements with explanatory sentences. Avoid a sequence of
  uniformly short, slogan-like lines.
- Consumer-facing target: approximately Grade 8-10 or equivalent plain language.

## Lexical rules

- Favor concrete language: "na prática", "funciona assim", "o problema aqui
  é", "o que realmente importa", and "é aqui que entra" when natural.
- "O ponto é" is optional vocabulary, not a required summary label or quota.
- Prefer verbs such as ajustar, construir, aplicar, estruturar, resolver, and
  melhorar.
- Explain technical terms on first use.
- Avoid filler, vague intensifiers, motivational hype, and excessive adjectives.

## Headline patterns

- Favor clear search-intent structures such as "Como...", "Por que...", "O que
  realmente...", and direct questions that match the reader's concern.
- Use numbered headlines only when the article genuinely contains a finite,
  useful sequence.
- Avoid unsupported superlatives, emotional clickbait, miracle framing, and
  guaranteed-result language.

## Voice profile

- **Seriousness**: calm and serious, with warmth; humor is rare and gentle.
- **Formality**: semi-formal and conversational, never slang-heavy or academic.
- **Respect**: strongly respectful and non-judgmental.
- **Enthusiasm**: measured and matter-of-fact; confidence comes from clarity and
  evidence, not excitement.

## Reference patterns

- Problem to insight: "O problema não é falta de esforço. É falta de direção."
- Calm authority: "Eu já vi esse cenário várias vezes. E quase sempre o erro
  está nos mesmos pontos." Use only when this accurately represents the author.
- Pragmatic contrast: "Simples não significa fácil."
- Direct CTA: "Se você quer começar por uma etapa concreta, comece por aqui."

Treat these as tonal examples, not phrases to repeat verbatim across posts.
