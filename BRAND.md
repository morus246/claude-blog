## Editorial Rules

### Always do

- Select the audience from the keyword, language, locale, and search intent
  before drafting.
- Validate the reader's experience before offering explanations or techniques.
- Explain why a technique may help and distinguish evidence from inference.
- Use scenarios that are realistic and anonymized; never fabricate a client
  story or imply personal clinical experience that was not provided.
- Structure practical posts as: problem, insight, safe technique, next step.
- Include a between-session practice only when it genuinely serves the topic.
- Match the CTA to intent: educational resource for informational searches;
  consultation for explicit treatment or local-service intent.
- Use "você" consistently in PT-BR; never switch casually to "tu" or "si".
- Prefer clarity before complexity.

### Never do

- Promise guaranteed, immediate, or universal outcomes.
- Present hypnotherapy as magic, mind control, or entertainment.
- Turn every topic into a generic numbered tips article.
- Force Jersey, finance-sector, or local-clinic framing into global content.
- Force the same distinctive-POV paragraph or CTA into every article.
- Use unexplained jargon, long unbroken text, or aggressive sales language.
- Diagnose the reader or advise changes to medication.

### Context-aware taboo phrases

- Avoid as promotional claims: "cura garantida", "resultado imediato",
  "nunca mais vai sentir ansiedade", "segredo", "hack milagroso",
  "garantido 100%", "cure yourself", "instant results", "magic solution",
  "imperdível", and "não perca".
- "Incrível" and "revolucionário" require specific evidence and should remain
  rare.
- A taboo term is permitted in quotation, negation, myth correction, source
  discussion, or a question that explicitly debunks the claim. Do not flag the
  term without evaluating its sentence-level context.

### Required disclosures

- Results from CBT and hypnotherapy vary by person and context.
- Educational content does not replace professional clinical assessment.
- Health or YMYL claims require inline sources appropriate to the claim.
- Crisis-related content must direct readers to appropriate urgent support.
